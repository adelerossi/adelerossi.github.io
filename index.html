The Fork, Redesigning the App — a UX case study.
UX Improvement and UI Re-design.

Read more

Brief — Redesigning and rethinking the The Fork app’s interface to improve usability and increase usage through research based on user needs and habits. TheFork is an online restaurant booking platform. Along with the case study, the following factors are the points we focused on.

Research — We set a boundary to design a simple solution. We interpreted possible solutions suitable for users.

User Target — Tourists, Students, Business Men, Couples, Standard Users.

Potential Users Interviews — We selected different types of people as users, to understand their pain points and daily challenges. We looked for common interests among users.

User Needs — Online booking; Finding restaurants nearby; Being able to communicate through reviews with team spirit; Photos, prices and more information regarding the restaurant; Possibility to personalize the restaurant research.

User Frustrations — Wasting time researching on the internet; No options to change reservations; Limited information about the restaurant; Long waits for confirmation.

Challenges — Unconsistent booking process; No proof of traditional cuisine; No option to change the reservation; No trust in people reviews.

Our Solutions — Easy and understandable booking’s flow; Saving time once at the restaurant and on using the App; User friendly approach while using the App.

Our Proposal —
1. Intelligent Map: possibility to choose a specific position and indicate it.
2. Pre-order: possibility to save time, pre-order and pay through the application.
3. TheForkCommunity: possibility to explore and discover reviews of bloggers, famous chefs, gastronomic professionals, discover new restaurants and have the opportunity to share your experience.
4. Insert Saved Research: the application can keep you updated according to your preferences and does not need to start searching from the outset.
5. Add User Feedback.

The Goal — stir passion, reach excellence of the research, increase loyalty, team spirit as an added value, satisfy the user's curiosity.

In collaboration with: Benedetta Diddi and Jessica Delgado
Mentor: Marco Catani
Year: 2020

‘Las Plantas’, Magazine realized for Marta Valpiana.
Editorial Design projects

Read more

‘Las Plantas’ is a magazine which examines the work of the Italian Visual Artist Marta Valpiana (Verona, 1993) and her project called ‘Neophyte’.
The artist analyzes the experience of plants in human life to understand her world, and consequently ours.
This magazine shows that boundaries between nature and culture, art and urban life, between human beings and wildlife can be much more indistinct than we are accustomed to think.

‘The truth is, we're colorful’- Video and Photos.
Color Psychology and Color Design.

Read more

Everything in our life creates feelings and emotions. If it was otherwise, nothing would make sense: each gesture, action, creation, and each experience. Moreover, our sensibility to colors establishes emotions. Visible reality itself is colored, in all of the aspects that surround us, colors evoke emotions and vibrations. Man, seen as both a natural being and social being, every day experiences emotions that the color evokes, witnessed through history, evolution and his ownerships.
This is the premise addressed for the statement ‘The Truth Is, We’re Colorful’. The project is about the psychology of colors, more specifically about colors’ symbolism and how we use them in our everyday life.
Color is a daily mystery, unique to each person. For this reason I have decided to analyze it and  then translate it into images that evoke such feelings.

This was my thesis project for my degree in New Technologies for Art at the Academy of Fine Arts. It was realized through investigation and the intersection between different fields of research such as history, philosophy, art, psychology and design. It was all made possible thanks to the Italian guidance of Francesca Valan, an Italian Color Designer based in Milan.
Take a look at the video and at the thesis research.

Year: 2017
